## Who We Are

We inherit the engineering discipline of **another-agent-skills**: mechanical enforcement, context budget, Guardian Pattern, verification over trust.

But we are not a clone. We are the **promotion flywheel**.

We believe the gap between "building a great product" and "people knowing about it" is not creativity — it's process. Good marketing doesn't require a big budget, a big team, or a big personality. It requires **disciplined execution**: research, create, distribute, measure, iterate.

We make agents that do the work. Not just advise on it.

---

## Why We Exist

AI agents can build entire applications in minutes. But when the code ships, the builder faces a harder problem: **nobody knows it exists**.

Marketing tools are fragmented. Each platform has its own rules, its own formats, its own APIs. The result is that great products fail because their creators can't — or won't — promote them.

We exist because **the hardest part of shipping a product shouldn't be telling people about it**.

---

## What We Believe

### 1. Generation is solved. Distribution is the new bottleneck.

AI can write copy, generate images, and script videos. The hard part is getting that content to the right platform, in the right format, at the right time. Our skills generate AND distribute.

### 2. The best marketing is specific, not generic.

Generic SaaS language ("streamline your workflow") is noise. Great marketing comes from product-specific angles, customer language, and authentic positioning. Every skill we build must produce output that feels **made for this exact product**.

### 3. Copy is not prompting. It's craft.

Writing copy that converts requires market research, audience understanding, persuasion psychology, and platform-specific rules. Our skills encode this knowledge — they don't just template-fill.

### 4. Multi-format beats single-format.

A product launch needs a video, a social post, an email, and a carousel. Separate tools for each create fragmentation. One skill, one input, multiple formats — that's the architecture.

### 5. The agent works. The human decides.

We never publish without approval. We never commit without consent. The Guardian Pattern applies to marketing as much as to code. The agent generates drafts. The human approves distribution.

### 6. Quality over quantity.

45 skills that advise are less valuable than 8 skills that deliver. We will never chase skill count. Every skill must generate a tangible, distributable asset.

### 7. Mechanical enforcement applies to marketing too.

Marketing content has quality gates: tone compliance, platform limits, brand voice, reading time, character counts. These can be checked mechanically — and they should be.

### 8. Context is still finite.

Same principle. SKILL.md <250 lines. References loaded on-demand. No skill dumps all marketing knowledge in one file.

### 9. The best launch is an ongoing conversation.

Marketing is not a one-time blast. It's a cycle: research → create → publish → listen → iterate. Our skills are designed for this cycle, not for one-shot campaigns.

### 10. We compete on depth, not breadth.

We don't build a CRO skill. We don't build an ads management skill. We don't build a pricing skill. We build the **promotion flywheel**: from product positioning to published content. That's our lane.

## What We Will NEVER Do

- **Never generate without context.** Every skill reads `product-marketing.md` first. No output without understanding the product, audience, and voice.
- **Never publish without approval.** Distribution is always a DECISION POINT. The agent schedules drafts; the human confirms.
- **Never silence the brand.** Generic copy is worse than no copy. Every output must pass brand voice compliance.
- **Never compete on breadth.** We will not become a "marketing department in a box." We stay in the promotion flywheel.
- **Never fake a win.** If an API fails, if a render breaks, if a distribution channel is down — TOOL_GAP. "Did not publish" is honest. "Looks like it published" is not.
- **Never abandon the non-developer.** Our output must be usable by someone who doesn't know what a CLI is. If they want automation, we provide it. If they want copy-paste, we provide that too.
- **Never add quantity over quality.** 8 skills that ship beats 45 that advise.

---

## Honest Limitations

Our skills generate marketing content. They do not guarantee results.

- **We can write the email. We cannot make people open it.**
- **We can generate the video. We cannot make it go viral.**
- **We can research the audience. We cannot replace talking to customers.**
- **We can check brand voice. We cannot invent a brand identity that doesn't exist.**

The agent is a force multiplier, not a replacement for human marketing judgment. The best results come from an agent that drafts and a human who directs.

## When Values Conflict

| Conflict | Winner | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Speed vs. Safety | **Safety** | A bad post is harder to delete than to schedule carefully. |
| Automation vs. Control | **Control** | The agent drafts. The human publishes. Always. |
| Breadth vs. Depth | **Depth** | One skill that generates 4 formats beats 4 skills that each generate 1. |
| Generic vs. Specific | **Specific** | Generic content damages brand trust. |
| Advice vs. Delivery | **Delivery** | "Here's the finished asset" beats "Here's what you should do." |
| Features vs. Enforcement | **Enforcement** | A format without quality gates is a liability. |

---

## How We Measure Ourselves

Not by stars. Not by skill count.

By these metrics:

1. **Outputs generated** — How many finished assets per session?
2. **Distribution rate** — How often does generated content get published?
3. **Brand voice pass rate** — How often does output pass tone compliance?
4. **Gate pass rate** — How often do mechanical gates catch issues?
5. **User override frequency** — How often does the human bypass our suggestions? (Lower is better.)
