# Source Guides — Per-Platform Research Playbooks

## Reddit Mining

**Best for:** Unfiltered customer language, pain points, objections.

**Subreddit discovery:**
- Search `site:reddit.com "your product category"` to find relevant subs
- Look for: `r/<industry>`, `r/<role>`, `r/SaaS`, `r/startups`

**Search operators:**
- `site:reddit.com "I wish" + "product category"`
- `site:reddit.com "frustrated with" + "alternative"`
- `site:reddit.com "recommend" + "product type"`
- `site:reddit.com/r/<sub> "how do you" + "problem"`

**What to extract:** Verbatim pain descriptions, alternative mentions, switching triggers, emotional language.

## G2 / Capterra Reviews

**Best for:** Competitive positioning, feature gaps, satisfaction drivers.

**Strategy:**
- Sort by "most recent" not "most helpful" (recent = current product state)
- Read 3-star reviews (most balanced — pros AND cons)
- Extract: what users love, what they hate, what they switched FROM

**Filters:** Use "Enterprise" filter for B2B, "SMB" for small business.

## Hacker News

**Best for:** Developer/technical audience insights, objections, alternatives.

**Search:** `site:news.ycombinator.com "Ask HN" + "your problem space"`
- "Ask HN" threads are gold for unprompted problem language
- "Show HN" comments reveal what technical users actually value

## App Store Reviews

**Best for:** B2C / consumer product insights.

**Strategy:**
- Focus on 1-3 star reviews (they contain frustrations, not praise)
- Extract: what broke the experience, what they expected, what they compare to
- Look for emotional language: "terrible", "frustrating", "love but..."

## LinkedIn

**Best for:** B2B trigger events, professional language, decision-maker insights.

**Search:**
- Look for posts about "switching from [competitor]" or "evaluating tools for"
- Comments on competitor posts often contain objection language
- Job postings reveal: what skills they value, what problems they're hiring for
