# Brand Voice Guide

Extracted as part of `product-marketing` skill. Read by all generation skills to ensure tone compliance.

## Voice Dimensions

| Dimension | Spectrum | Example Brands |
|-----------|----------|---------------|
| Formal ↔ Casual | Professional ↔ Conversational | Apple vs Wendy's |
| Serious ↔ Playful | Factual ↔ Humorous | IBM vs Old Spice |
| Respectful ↔ Irreverent | Polite ↔ Bold | NYT vs Barstool |
| Enthusiastic ↔ Reserved | High energy ↔ Calm | Red Bull vs Mailchimp |
| Informational ↔ Inspirational | Data ↔ Emotion | HBR vs Nike |
| Simple ↔ Complex | Grade 5 ↔ Grade 12 | Dollar Shave Club vs Economist |
| Expert ↔ Peer | Authority ↔ Relatable | McKinsey vs Duolingo |

## Tone Mapping by Channel

| Channel | Adjustment | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Website | Core voice, polished | First impression |
| Blog | Educational, slightly formal | Trust-building |
| Email | Warm, conversational | One-to-one channel |
| Twitter/X | Sharp, quick, witty | Real-time, character-limited |
| LinkedIn | Professional, authoritative | Business context |
| Instagram | Visual-first, casual | Emotional connection |
| TikTok | Raw, authentic, less polished | Platform values authenticity |
