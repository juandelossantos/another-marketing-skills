# Tone System

7 presets adapted from /brag. Tone affects scripting energy, pacing, typography, and transitions.

## Presets

| Tone | Energy | Language | Best for |
|------|--------|----------|----------|
| default | Playful, clean | Direct, positive | General product launches |
| polished | Elegant, serious | Formal, confident | B2B / enterprise / professional |
| chaotic | Fast, loud, aggressive | Bold, exaggerated | Consumer apps / fun products |
| deadpan | Calm, dry, understated | Minimal, flat irony | Anti-hype / developer tools |
| cinematic | Dramatic, trailer-scale | Big claims, high stakes | Major releases / milestones |
| app-store | Smooth, feature-clean | Benefit-driven, clear | Product updates / feature drops |
| warm | Friendly, personal | Conversational, inclusive | Community / email / social |

## Brand Voice Integration

Each tone must pass through the brand voice dimensions from `product-marketing.md` Section 10:

```
Voice dimensions: [formal↔casual] [serious↔playful] [respectful↔irreverent]
Tone shift per channel: website/blog/email/social
Words to use: [from product-marketing.md]
Words to avoid: [from product-marketing.md]
```

The tone preset adjusts energy and pacing. The brand voice chart keeps vocabulary and personality consistent.

## Channel Tone Shifts

| Channel | Apply |
|---------|-------|
| LinkedIn | Shift 1 step toward professional |
| Twitter | Shift 1 step toward casual/witty |
| Instagram | Visual-first, tone takes second priority |
| Email | Shift 1 step toward warm/personal |
| Video | Full tone preset applies |
| Ad copy | Shift 1 step toward direct/benefit |
